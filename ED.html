What is an ED Worksheet?

A core engineering design tool used to plan, evaluate, and document the design process.

📋

Structured Planning

Organises all design information — requirements, functions, constraints, and decisions — in one structured document.

⚖

Trade-off Analysis

Helps the team evaluate competing design choices by weighing performance, cost, weight, and usability.

🎯

Goal Alignment

Keeps the entire team aligned on what the design must achieve and what boundaries it must stay within.

📝

Design Record

Creates a traceable record of every design decision, making it easier to revisit, review, and improve.

The Design Process

How the ED Worksheet fits into the broader engineering design cycle.

1. Problem Definition

Clearly state what needs to be designed, who it's for, and what problem it solves — the foundation of any good design.

2. Requirements Gathering

List all objectives (what the design should do) and constraints (what it must not exceed) to set the design boundaries.

3. Concept Generation

Brainstorm multiple possible solutions and evaluate each one against the defined requirements and constraints.

4. Decision Making

Use trade-off matrices and scoring to select the best design concept based on objective criteria.

5. Specification & Documentation

Document all final decisions, component choices, and design parameters in the ED Worksheet for review and implementation.
